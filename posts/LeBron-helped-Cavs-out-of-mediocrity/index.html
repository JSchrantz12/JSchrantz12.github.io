The savior of the Cleveland Cavaliers franchise, LeBron James joined the NBA in the early 2000s. As one of the most hyped NBA prospects ever, the Akron, Ohio native has shocked the world by living up to the ridiculous expectations that were put on him as an 18-year-old kid right out of high school. His ability to dominate other legends of the game has led to a number of Cavaliers franchise-highs. However, when James was in other destinations winning championships, the lows of NBA basketball returned to Cleveland almost instantly.

Prior to being drafted in the 2003 NBA Draft, LeBron’s future organization located along Lake Erie was down and out. However, once he stepped foot in Cleveland for his first NBA experience, things changed in the wins category for the Cavaliers right along with him. The Cavs saw a huge rise in success for years, reaching the playoffs multiple times, and even the NBA Finals once in 2007. However, when James left to take his talents to South Beach, the Wine and Gold fell right back down to the bottom of the barrel in the NBA. It wasn’t until the King returned home did the Cavaliers have significant playoff success again. James then left again for Los Angeles, and once again, Cleveland fell to mediocrity.

LeBron James is known for being ball-dominant offensively. He is usually at his best when he can pick apart defenses while having control of the basketball. This idea is reflected in Cleveland’s offensive efficiency stats. Their team true shooting percentage when LeBron was a Cavalier superstar was generally higher than any other season in Cavaliers basketball where James was somewhere else.

The King has been one of the best finishers around the rim in the NBA for over two decades. His ability to drive with the basketball and use his strength to power through biggest and strongest that the NBA has to offer has helped Cleveland reach great heights as a team. The Cavaliers have generally shot their highest field goal percentages from inside 3-feet away from the rim when LeBron was driving “Right Down Euclid,” as the immortal words of former Cleveland Cavaliers broadcaster, Fred McLeod would say.

The Cavaliers have had the pleasure of having one of the greatest of all-time lead their franchise multiple times. LeBron James has brought unprecedented success to the Cavaliers. He led the Wine and Gold to their only NBA Championship in 2016, and brought respect to a franchise that was down and out before he stepped foot into the NBA. The Cavs future looks bright with their group of young players in 2023, such as Darius Garland, Evan Mobley and Donovan Mitchell. However, it will be very difficult for any future Cavaliers to ever live up to the success of the home-grown superstar, LeBron James.
